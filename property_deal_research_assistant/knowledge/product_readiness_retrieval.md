# Property Deal Retrieval Playbook

## Useful Retrieval Queries

- Which candidate properties, buyer constraints, financing assumptions, and diligence questions were supplied?
- What evidence supports ranking one deal ahead of another?
- Which underwriting claims require public-record, lender, broker, inspection, or rent-roll verification?

## Evidence Checklist

Use the provided acquisition criteria, market thesis, candidate property facts, debt assumptions, rent estimates, known issues, and user diligence questions as source evidence. Do not invent comps, tax assessments, inspection results, vacancy, insurance quotes, zoning, or MLS facts.

Compare each deal on price, units, current rent, market-rent gap, renovation reserve, debt service sensitivity, lease rollover, physical risks, legal encumbrances, and exit-risk. Separate modeled upside from verified facts. A promising deal with unverified rent growth should be ranked as "conditional" and assigned diligence tasks.

## Output Guidance

The memo should include ranked opportunities, base/downside assumptions, financing blockers, diligence checklist, source gaps, and a no-action warning until documents are reviewed. Good next steps are requests for rent roll, T12, insurance quote, inspection, title/easement review, and lender term sheet confirmation.
