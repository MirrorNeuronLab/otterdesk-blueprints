# Purchase Research Review Task

## Goal

Review a purchase research packet and prepare a deep, generic purchase analysis for human decision-making. The purchase may be a home, rental, vehicle, trip, service, subscription, equipment, software, collectible, or any other good or service.

## Instructions

- Think through the decision in layers: what is being bought, whether it fits the need, what alternatives exist, what it really costs over the decision horizon, how reliable or safe it is, what terms and obligations apply, and what could go wrong after purchase.
- Use only the supplied deterministic evidence, retrieved local context, and cited public-source records. Treat retrieved guidance as a checklist, never as proof.
- Distinguish observed facts, corroborated facts, conflicts, assumptions, unknowns, stale information, and next evidence requests.
- Check the one-time price plus taxes, fees, financing, delivery, installation, subscriptions, maintenance, insurance, energy or usage costs, switching costs, deposits, cancellation costs, and likely exit or resale costs when relevant.
- Check fit, quality, durability, compatibility, accessibility, safety, privacy, security, regulatory or eligibility constraints, seller/provider reliability, delivery or timing, warranty, returns, cancellation, support, and alternatives. Skip irrelevant dimensions explicitly rather than silently assuming they do not matter.
- Stress-test the user’s budget, must-haves, priorities, and time horizon. Surface the strongest reason to buy and the strongest reason to wait or avoid.
- Compare alternatives only when the supplied evidence makes them meaningfully comparable; state the comparison basis and any non-comparable fields.
- Keep the recommendation constrained to `buy`, `consider`, `wait`, `avoid`, or `insufficient_evidence`.
- Preserve source references and retrieval timestamps for volatile facts.

## Restrictions

- Do not invent prices, availability, policies, fees, condition, legal status, safety, compatibility, performance, or travel requirements.
- Do not turn a generic checklist into a claim that a specific item satisfies it.
- Do not expose private local evidence in public queries.
- Do not buy, book, pay, submit an offer or application, or contact a seller/provider.
