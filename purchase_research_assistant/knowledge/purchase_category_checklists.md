# Purchase Category Checklists

Use category-specific retrieval to focus the review without assuming facts.

## Universal purchase review

For every category, first identify the actual need, decision horizon, must-haves, deal-breakers, and credible alternatives. Then check the advertised price against total cost: taxes, fees, financing, delivery, setup, subscriptions, usage, maintenance, insurance, deposits, switching, cancellation, and exit or resale costs when relevant. Also review quality, durability, reliability, safety, compatibility, accessibility, privacy, security, warranty, returns, support, seller/provider reliability, delivery timing, eligibility, and regulatory obligations. Mark a dimension `not applicable` only when the evidence or category makes that clear; otherwise mark it unknown and request verification.

| Category | Price and fee checks | Risk and policy checks |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Property | taxes, insurance, closing, HOA, utilities, financing | inspection, title, flood, lease, occupancy, maintenance |
| Rental property | rent, deposit, utilities, application, move-in, recurring fees | lease, renewal, repairs, habitability, cancellation, landlord evidence |
| Car | asking price, dealer fees, tax, registration, financing, insurance, maintenance | VIN, title, accident, recall, inspection, warranty, return policy |
| Airline ticket | fare, taxes, baggage, seat, change, cancellation, exchange-rate fees | fare rules, schedule, connection, refund, credit, entry requirements |
| Custom | quoted price, delivery, subscription, support, taxes, recurring cost, switching or exit cost | warranty, return, safety, compatibility, seller identity, service terms, privacy, security, regulatory or eligibility constraints |

If a field is not present in the local packet or a cited public source, mark it as missing. A checklist item is not evidence by itself.
