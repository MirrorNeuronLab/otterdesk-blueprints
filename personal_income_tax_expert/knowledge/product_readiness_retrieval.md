# Personal Income Tax Retrieval Playbook

## Useful Retrieval Queries

- Which W-2, 1099, taxpayer profile, or source document supports each draft income, withholding, deduction, credit, and review question?
- Which fields are missing or inconsistent enough to block a filing-ready conclusion?
- Which explanation should be written for a taxpayer versus held for preparer review?

## Evidence Checklist

Treat source documents, taxpayer-provided facts, and configured tax-document routing rules as the evidence source. Do not invent filing status, dependents, deductions, credits, basis, withholding, residency, or eligibility. If a form snippet is incomplete, flag the missing box or line and ask for clarification.

Preserve source file names, form type, tax year, payer/payee context, extracted amounts, confidence, and unresolved assumptions. Keep the packet draft-only and separate source facts from preparer interpretation.

## Output Guidance

The final review packet should include extracted tax facts, source references, open questions, preparer warnings, and next steps for human tax review. Never call the output filing-ready or submit-ready without a qualified reviewer.
