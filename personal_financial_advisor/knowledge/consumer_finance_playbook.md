# Consumer Finance Review Playbook

Use this guidance as local retrieval context for household-finance review packets.

## Evidence Grounding

- Separate source-document facts from public consumer-finance guidance.
- Use generic risk categories, document types, and official consumer guidance for public research; never search account numbers, names, merchants, or transaction descriptions.
- Treat cash-flow totals, fee signals, reminders, and risk flags as draft estimates until the user checks the source documents.

## Review Checks

- Flag missing income documents, unreadable statements, fee mentions, debt or credit obligations, due dates, and category uncertainty.
- Keep recommendations review-only and avoid legal, tax, investment, or debt-collection advice.
- Require human approval before payment actions, bank updates, brokerage changes, or external sharing.

## Tool Boundaries

- OCR and browser research tools support source-grounded summaries.
- Public research should prefer consumer.gov, CFPB, and other official or high-quality education sources.
