# Personal Financial Advisor Retrieval Playbook

## Useful Retrieval Queries

- Which source statement, OCR snippet, or transaction row supports each income, expense, balance, fee, or obligation?
- What recurring cash-flow patterns are visible without exposing sensitive account details?
- Which recommendations are education-only and require a qualified human review?

## Evidence Checklist

Use source documents, normalized transactions, account summaries, consumer-finance guidance, and user constraints as evidence. Do not invent income, balances, rates, account numbers, investment returns, credit score, tax facts, or eligibility for products. Redact or avoid account numbers, taxpayer IDs, and unnecessary merchant details.

Classify recurring income, fixed expenses, variable expenses, debt service, fees, emergency-fund coverage, unusual transactions, and missing documents. Separate budget observations from financial advice. Browser research should favor official or widely trusted consumer guidance and should not send private transaction details to search.

## Output Guidance

The final report should include cash-flow summary, evidence-backed risks, education-oriented options, missing information, and a review checklist. Avoid personalized product recommendations or investment orders; use "consider discussing with a qualified advisor" for actions with legal, tax, credit, or investment implications.
