# Drug Discovery Retrieval Playbook

## Useful Retrieval Queries

- What target profile, assay plan, candidate set, potency claims, and safety filters were supplied?
- Which candidate has the strongest evidence for the desired therapeutic hypothesis?
- Which claims need literature, assay, ADME, selectivity, manufacturability, or safety confirmation?

## Evidence Checklist

Treat all sample compounds, targets, and assay outcomes as hypothesis-support material only. Do not imply clinical efficacy, patient benefit, regulatory readiness, or experimental validation unless explicit source evidence is present. If potency, selectivity, exposure, toxicity, or novelty data is missing, mark it as a required experiment.

Rank candidates by fit to the target product profile, evidence quality, selectivity, safety liabilities, assay readiness, and next experiment. Surface common blockers such as hERG risk, broad off-target activity, CYP interactions, poor permeability fit, unclear disease linkage, or insufficient target validation.

## Output Guidance

The final report should contain candidate ranking, evidence table, uncertainty, go/no-go rationale, and the next non-clinical experiment for each candidate. Keep language preclinical and review-only. Recommend expert scientific review before synthesis, wet-lab work, IP decisions, or investment action.
