# Multi-Agent Research Playbook

This playbook uses specialized roles to create useful disagreement. The topic finder creates a constrained research plan. The literature reviewer discovers sources and updates an evidence ledger. The idea generator proposes alternatives rather than one preferred answer. The skeptic attacks causal leaps, missing controls, and unstated assumptions. The proximity reviewer looks for conceptual overlap and limits novelty language. The experiment designer writes falsifiable tests. The code, benchmark, and result-analysis roles plan reproducible evaluation. The paper writer drafts the research brief, and the meta-reviewer audits the whole packet.

## Candidate hypothesis format

Each candidate must include:

1. A short statement of the mechanism or intervention.
2. Its predicted observable result.
3. Supporting evidence references and the strength of that support.
4. A competing explanation or skeptical critique.
5. A disconfirming observation.
6. An experiment, simulation, or analysis concept, including baseline and control.
7. Required approvals, data, and safety or ethics constraints.

The ranking is a triage aid, not a claim of truth, novelty, priority, or feasibility. A candidate with weak or conflicting evidence should normally be returned for more research rather than polished into a stronger-sounding claim.

## Iteration policy

The workflow allows one bounded pass per role. It may revise a candidate when a critique identifies a precise evidence gap or failed prediction, but it does not recursively debate until it appears certain. Human review decides whether to obtain more evidence, change a research plan, authorize an experiment, or draft an external-facing work.
