# Research Governance and Retrieval

Local knowledge retrieval is a navigation aid. It may surface relevant passages, but cited source material and approved local records remain the evidence of record. If an embedding service is unavailable, the workflow falls back to lexical retrieval and records that condition in the artifact.

Public research queries must contain only sanitized research-goal, domain, and question text. Never include credentials, raw confidential notes, participant data, protected health information, unpublished proprietary results, contact details, or restricted datasets in a public query. Respect source terms, robots restrictions, rate limits, access controls, and copyright. Do not bypass a CAPTCHA or login wall.

Human review is required before live experiments, system changes, participant contact, regulated or clinical interpretation, publication, public claims, or any safety-critical decision. A research packet can recommend a review action or a request for more evidence, but it cannot authorize itself. When an input topic could affect people, equipment, the environment, or a regulated process, record the approval dependency and stop short of operational instructions.

The review ledger should state who must validate the evidence, what evidence remains missing, and which action is blocked. This keeps the research workflow useful even when it cannot safely complete the next real-world step.
